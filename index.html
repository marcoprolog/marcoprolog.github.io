About

I am currently a PhD fellow in the Center for Computer Games Research games at the IT University of Copenhagen.

I completed my Master degree in in Information Technology through the Media Technology and Games program of IT University of Copenhagen in June 2014. During my master studies I have developed a great interest in Procedural Content Generation for games, especially of music. My research at the time created the basis of what now is my PhD work: the expression of affective meaning through computer-generated music.

I have also developed different types of games (both large and small) during my education and in my spare time. I received my Bachelor degree in Computer Science from the Universit√† Statale di Milano in 2011, in which I specialized in artificial intelligence and mathematical logic.

My PhD research investigates the expression of moods and the affective effect this mood-expressive music can have on the listener and applying this research to games: the final objective is to create a system where, by using a cognitive model of the player, we would be able to identify the player's emotional state and be able to reinforce or manipulate it through the use of mood-expressive music to improve user experience.

What we hope to achieve is creating better immersive experiences (reinforcement of current emotional state and playstyle) and help the designers create experiences where the players are put in a specific emotional state (manipulation).

Mood Expressive Music Generator

This generator has been developed during part of my Master and the start of my PhD, it has been realized using PD (aka Pure Data), a real-time graphical programming environment for audio, video, and graphical processing. The generated music is played by 3 instruments (synthesizers) and a drum machine. The system consists of five random number generators: four of these numbers will be converted into notes for the instruments and the drums, whilethe last one is used as a sound effect controller.

It creates randomic music that can express different moods by manipulating a relatively small set of variables that are indipendent from harmony and melody. The resulting music sounds very ambient.

Currently I'm developing a new generator that will probably not use PD and hopefully will be able to express a bigger musical space and produce more interesting music.

SMUG: Scientific Music Generator

Music is based on the real world. Composers use their day-to-day lives as inspiration to create rhythm and lyrics. Procedural music generators are capable of creating good quality pieces, and while some already use the world as inspiration, there is still much to be explored in this. We have developed a system to generate lyrics and melodies from real-world data, in particular from academic papers. Through this we wanted to create a playful experience and establish a novel way of generating content (textual and musical) that could be applied to other domains, in particular to games. For melody generation, we have used a novel approach to Markov chains evolution.

Our system takes in input a PDF of an academic paper and outputs lyrics and relative musical melody.

Four Part Harmony Co-Evolution

This project evolves four part harmonies, either with a given bass or free. The evolution uses the Multiple Worlds Model defined by Joseph Brown.

The model makes distinct populations evolve independently, but when calculating the fitness function requires the individuals from the various populations to work together. In our case we have a population for each of the four voices and the fitness function is calculated by considering the voices singing together.

This approach leads to speciation, where each of the voices evolve in best way to work together with the other ones.

Backpack Adventure

This game was developed during Nordic Game Jam 2015.

You play as the backpack of an adventurer, organizing loot and helping him overcoming enemies.

As the hero fights monsters and finds loot, the backpack (you) need to make sure he has space for all the new items, trash the stuff that he doesn't need, help him by giving him weapons, armor, potions, and food when he is fighting. When merchants pass by you get a chance to sell the valuables and upgrade the hero, so you can get more loot.
